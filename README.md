Although sensor fusion consistently improves monitoring accuracy by combining signals such as vibration, acoustic emission, and temperature, most fusion models are still validated in narrow settings—often one material, one process, or one controlled dataset—so their real portability is unclear. When the material changes (for example, steel to titanium), the cutting physics and thermal load shift the signal patterns, and many fusion systems end up learning **material-specific fingerprints** rather than true wear-related features, causing performance to collapse. Very little research designs fusion explicitly for **cross-material generalisation** (domain shift) with a clear benchmark setup like “train on steel, test on titanium,” and reporting is often weak on uncertainty and failure conditions. This leaves a clear gap for a **material-agnostic feature fusion framework** that can keep predictions stable across different materials without constant retraining or re-tuning.
